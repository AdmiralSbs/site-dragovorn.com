Nationalism - Pride in one's nation that can be used for good and/or bad.
    Causes - The limiting of liberties of a nation.
Conservatism v. Liberalism - Traditional political power vs. changes of/in political power.
Nationalism leads to Imperialism or vise versa.
Constructive Nationalism - The construction of new nations.
Destructive Nationalism - Contries and/empires are reduced or broken up.
Romanticism - Rejection of the industrial world and it's dehumanizing components. A return nature was idealized. Conventional society was rejected. (Byronic Hero)
Congress of Vienna - 1815
    Goal/Purpose - "Fix" Europe after the defeat of Napoleon Bonaparte. Restore old boundaries and original "legitimate" monarchs.
    Member Nations - Britian, Russian, Austria and France-- "Balance of Power"
    Impact on Italy and Germany - They were ruled by foreign governments rather than their own.
Revolutions of 1848 - Numerous revolutions that took place throughout Europe that year.
    Causes - Challeneges of traditional power (growth of democracy), failed economics, demand for more rights (very heavy period of liberalism)
    Consiquences - Some failed.
Italian Unification - Italy became a unified nation with Cavour leading from the north (Sardinia) and Garibaldi leading from the south (Sicily).
    Division of Italy as a result of the Congress of Vienna - Northern Italy was ruled by Austria, while Southern Italy ruled by the Bourbons.
    Kingdom of Piedmont-Sardinia - Place where the whole unification movement began.
    Significant Leaders:
        Cavour - Newspaper edittor who reall made it happen in the North.
        Victor Emmanuel - Becomes the king of the newly unified Italy in 1870
        Garibaldi - Led unification in Southern Italy with his red shirt army
        Mazzini - Led the unification movement as the "social networker" ...Young Italy (secret society)
    Constitutional Monarchy - A nation ruled by a king/queen that has a consitution (some form of individual rights and liberties)
German Unification:
    Impact of he Congress of Vienna - Ruled by Austria
    German Confederation - 35 states ruled by Austria
    Zollverein - Economic trade union created in the German Confederation
    Prussia - Leaders of the whole unification movement for Germany
    Significant Leaders:
    Bismarck - Orchestrated the Unification of Germany
    Wilhelm I - Became the king of the newly unified Germany
    Wars:
        War against Denmark - Teamed up with their enemy Austria to take the Schleswig and Holstein
        Austro-Prussian War - (seven weeks war) Prussia succeeded in getting Austria out of their hair
        Franco-Prussian War - Led to the defeat of France and the completion of the German Unification process. 1871
